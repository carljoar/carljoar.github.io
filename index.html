Research

Interests

Geometric and microlocal analysis, partial differential equations, spectral theory, functional analysis, geometric flows on manifolds, and applications.

Current projects include:

  • Explaining the vast intra-specific diversity of microbes and at the same time unify several theories from ecology. To this end, we develop a game theoretical model, and we find all optimal strategies.
  • Developing a general framework for regularized gradient flows induced by Lie-Fréchet group actions on Riemannian manifolds, and implementing diffeomorphic action on smooth functions on the 2-torus regularized by the metric deformation in Python.

    • Ways to introduce Active Learning (cont. updated):

  • State a theorem using its intuitive formulation. Give an example, e.g., by application. Then let the students specify the details or the missing points of the theorem.
  • Create a summary of the lecture together with the class. Make sure to say to the students that you will put it online. Put it online!
